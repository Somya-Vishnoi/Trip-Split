COLLEGE FINAL PROJECT PRESENTATION

TRIPSPLIT OPTIMIZER

Student Travel Budget & Route Optimization Engine tailored for Indian multi-city travel with automated group expense tracking.

DEPARTMENT OF COMPUTER SCIENCE & ENGINEERING
PROBLEM STATEMENT

Travel Planning & Financial Challenges

Budget Allocation Confusion

Students struggle to distinguish between total group budgets vs per-person shares, leading to critical miscalculations during initial planning.

High Manual Research Effort

Designing multi-day travel itineraries across Indian cities manually takes hours of research with unpredictable venue and hotel costs.

Generic Non-Tailored Planners

Existing booking engines recommend high-end commercial spots without catering to student pricing constraints or regional transport choices.

Post-Trip Financial Friction

Shared group trips often suffer from unorganized bill tracking, resulting in complex and awkward debt settlements after returning.

THE TRIPSPLIT SOLUTION

System Features & Intelligent Core

Dynamic Budget Partitioning

Automatically divides input budget across Transport, Stay, Food, Local Travel, and Buffer with real-time per-person breakdowns.

Budget-Free Sightseeing Logic

Sightseeing entry tickets are calculated as budget-free, ensuring rich attraction recommendations regardless of accommodation tier.

5 Comparative Itinerary Styles

Generates five distinct trip plans in parallel: Best Overall, Cheapest Trip, Slow & Relaxed, More Places, and Better Stay.

Group Expense Splitter

Features a built-in group bill manager with equal/custom splits, net settlement reduction algorithms, and visual charts.

ALGORITHM PIPELINE

Optimization Heuristics & Knapsack Execution

Phase 1: Bounding Box & Geocoding
Converts target city inputs into lat/lon coordinates. Applies a dense 5km radius bounding box cap for metro cities (Delhi, Mumbai) to optimize search latency.
Phase 2: Venue Utility Scoring
Classifies query results into Stays, Dining, and Attractions with utility scores (0-100) and cost estimates tailored for student travel.
Phase 3: Constrained Partitioning
Allocates funds across fixed categories (Transport, Accommodation, Food, Local Travel) with a minimum 10% safety buffer.
Phase 4: Sequential Day Slotting
Slots attractions sequentially into Morning, Afternoon, Evening, and Night timeline buckets, producing structured day-by-day plans.
